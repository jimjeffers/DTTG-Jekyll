Hot on the trail of my last post I have performed even further resource to get another look at how spending has been managed during the last five presidents over the last thirty years. In this case I referenced the following source:

  • Table 3.1. Government Current Receipts and Expenditures
  • I looked at the Current Receipts and the Current Expenditures for each year to calculate the growth rates.

Expenditures Never Decreased; They Only Grew Less

I think this is crucial when listening to political rhetoric. A lot of politicians say we need to decrease spending or are as bold to say that they promise to decrease spending. I'm not sure I would believe that. Analyzing the last thirty years of data we can see that the growth rate of current expenditures has never been negative. However, the rate at which they grew has fluctuated.

Conversely, the growth rate for current receipts was negative during the second two years of George W. Bush's presidency. These were the only two years out of the past thirty that government income actually shrank: -0.41% in 2001 and -4.96% in 2002. During these periods government expenditures grew at 6.07% in 2001 and 5.85% in 2002.

Spending Was Most Controlled During Clinton's Presidency

If we calculate the average annual growth rates of receipts and expenditures during the past five presidents terms it is very clear that government spending was best controlled while Clinton was in office.
